# GURUMBÉ CAPITAL — Website Structure

**Status:** Working Document  
**Purpose:** Site architecture, section logic, and copy direction  
**Version:** 0.1

---

## OVERVIEW

The website serves three audiences simultaneously — institutional LPs and family offices, retail investors, and tech-native / alternative allocators — while never compromising the brand's cultural depth or visual authority. The language is English, with Spanish woven in at key moments (section titles, epigraphs) to signal the corridor without alienating.

The site is not a pitch deck. It is a statement of presence.

## SECTION 2 — THE CORRIDOR

*¿Qué somos? / What We Are*

**Purpose:** Frame the civilizational thesis without overwhelming. This is the philosophical anchor.

**Structure:**
- Opening paragraph: the historical link (Gurumbé, flamenco, Bantu roots, Seville)
- The key assertion: Spain is the South Arrow of Europe — not a passive observer but a structural gateway
- The shift: from North-to-South extractive finance to South-to-South sovereign partnership
- The four focus countries: Kenya, Ghana, Namibia, Angola — brief, evocative, one sentence each

**Pull quote:**
> *"We do not construct a new relationship. We frame, claim, and deliver an existing space that history temporarily obscured."*

**Design note:** This section benefits from a split layout — text on one side, a rotating or static Adinkra symbol on the other. Solar gold accent on the symbol.

## SECTION 3 — WHY NOW

*La convergencia / The Convergence*

**Purpose:** The macro thesis. Why this corridor, why tokenization, why 2025–2026.

**Structure — three convergent forces:**

1. **The Demographic Fact**  
   Africa's population, digital adoption rate, and resource endowment make it the defining economic engine of the coming decades. This is not a risk to manage. It is a conviction to act on.

2. **The Capital Shift**  
   Capital pools are exiting dollar-denominated and equity-heavy positions. Currency competition is no longer theoretical. Tokenized real-world assets backed by physical African resources are emerging as a serious institutional answer.

3. **The Architectural Gap**  
   The infrastructure to direct European and global capital into African assets — legally structured, culturally credible, operationally grounded — does not yet exist in coherent form. That is what we are building.

**Design note:** Three columns or three stacked horizontal bands. Minimalist. Numbers or Adinkra symbols as section markers.

## SECTION 4 — WHAT WE BUILD

*Activos / Asset Classes*

**Purpose:** Concrete. Specific. Builds trust through clarity.

**Four asset classes — presented as a phased roadmap:**

### Phase 1 — Smart Real Estate *(The Roots)*
Tokenized African serviced and hospitality real estate. Title-backed. Smart contracts automate revenue-sharing and personal rights-of-use. An invitation to discover Africa while investing in its future.  
*Focus hubs: Nairobi, Accra, Windhoek, Luanda*

### Phase 2 — Decentralized Energy Infrastructure *(The Current)*
Distributed solar arrays and regional microgrids. Tokenized future energy cash flows. Energy as its own currency hedge. Africa's solar irradiation is a structural advantage — we convert it into investable yield.  
*Focus markets: Namibia, Ghana, Kenya*

### Phase 3 — VC / Venture Building *(The Builders)*
We act as internal venture builders. We co-invest alongside aligned partners, internalize operational responsibilities, and scale native technical talent across the corridor. We do not outsource key execution.

### Phase 4 — African Public Equities *(The Anchor)*
Strategic capital allocations into undervalued, high-yielding public equities on regional African stock exchanges. Portfolio liquidity and pricing anchor. Digital infrastructure mirrored in the legal entity.

**Design note:** Horizontal scrolling cards on mobile. Grid on desktop. Each asset class has its own Adinkra symbol or abstract icon. Phase labels are subtle but present.

## SECTION 5 — HOW WE WORK

*La arquitectura / The Architecture*

**Purpose:** Institutional credibility. Show the structure without burying the reader.

**Three-part explanation:**

### The Vehicle
A Holding Company in Spain (Iberia) acts as the primary interface for macro investors and sovereign dialogues. Asset-specific SPVs deploy beneath it — one per project, legally ring-fenced, structurally isolated.

### The Token
We never use the word "crypto." We use: *fractional digital issuance, programmable compliance architecture, direct asset-backed units.* Two levels of digital issuance: SPV-level (direct project exposure) and Holding-level (broad portfolio exposure).

### The Principles — Tokenization as a Binary Transition
Not an upgrade. A structural shift: from unit to unicity. Four properties that unlock new forms of capital:
- **Composability** — multiple instruments in one wrapper
- **Finality** — real-time settlement, no clearing-house risk
- **Fractionalization** — institutional assets in accessible tranches
- **Programmability** — automated yields, compliance, distribution

**Design note:** The corporate structure diagram from the Seed document can be rendered visually here — clean, minimal, dark background with solar gold lines. Not a org chart. An architectural drawing.

## SECTION 7 — THOUGHT LEADERSHIP

*El pensamiento / The Thinking*

**Purpose:** Establish intellectual authority. Signal that Gurumbé has a point of view — not just a product.

**Structure:**

### The Long Read (Featured)
One flagship essay, updated periodically. The first candidate:
- *"The Corridor Was Always There: Africa, Iberia, and the Architecture of South-to-South Capital"* — a 2,000–3,000 word piece drawing on the Seed document's civilizational thesis, the Gurumbé/flamenco historical root, and the tokenization infrastructure argument.

### The Archive
Three categories of shorter pieces:
1. **Capital & Infrastructure** — tokenization, RWA design, SPV architecture, the AfDB / AUA thesis
2. **The Corridor** — history, culture, geopolitics of the Spain-Africa-LatAm axis
3. **Operators' Notes** — on-the-ground field observations from Kenya, Ghana, Namibia, Angola

**Format options per piece:**
- Full essay (700–2,500 words)
- Perspective brief (300–500 words, more frequent)
- Annotated link (external piece + Gurumbé commentary)

**Design note:** Editorial magazine layout. Large headline, author, date, category tag. Reading time shown. No pagination clutter. The goal is to feel like a journal of record for the corridor — not a blog.

## SECTION 8 — CONTACT / ENGAGE

*Conversemos / Let's Talk*

**Purpose:** Low friction entry for the right conversations.

**Three entry points:**

1. **Investors** — I am interested in the asset pipeline / first close
2. **Partners** — I have operational or institutional alignment to explore
3. **Media & Speaking** — I want to platform this work

**Form:** Name, email, a free-text field ("Tell us what brought you here"), and a selector for the three entry points above. No phone number required. No mandatory fields beyond name and email.

## DESIGN SYSTEM NOTES

**Typography:**
- Headers: A historical serif (Cormorant Garamond, EB Garamond, or Playfair Display) — roots, permanence
- Body / Technical: A geometric mono or clean sans (DM Mono, or a refined grotesque) — precision, modern

**Palette:**
- Base: Deep Iberian charcoal (`#1a1612` or similar)
- Neutral: Warm Andalusian cream (`#e8dfc8`)
- Accent 1: African Ochre / Solar Gold (`#c8841d` or `#d4a017`)
- Accent 2: Terracotta / Clay (`#b5531d`)
- Rule lines: Gold at 15–20% opacity

**Motifs:**
- Adinkra symbols used sparingly as section markers and decorative elements — never decorative noise
- Grain overlay on hero and key sections (see Meridiano seed for reference)
- Architectural / structural diagrams in gold-on-dark for the SPV section

**Language:**
- English primary
- Spanish woven in for section labels, epigraphs, pull quotes — not translated, just present
- Occasional Bantu/West African reference where culturally earned (Gurumbé, Adinkra)
